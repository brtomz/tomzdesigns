Open Air Market

University of Arkansas: Advanced Design Camp - 2025
We were tasked with creating an open market space for the community. With an oddly shaped site plan, I wanted to honor the natural landscape and designed buildings with curved exteriors and made each space functional, integrating trees into benches, seating areas within the gardens and incorporating materials that were native to NW Arkansas.

Art

Summer Cherry

Original Size - 6" x 9" | Colored pencil on paper | 2024

Seasonal changes can affect our emotional state. Some people feel sadness in the winter months and some, like me, feel buoyed by the first signs of summer. Playing with shading techniques, I tried to convey the connection between a fresh cherry and the bright hope of summer’s arrrival.

Kendrick

Original Size - 4" x 4" | Pencil on paper | 2024

This was practice in sketching realistic eyes and trying to capture how emotions can be conveyed and whole conversations can happen wordlessly through a glance.

The Fifth Option

Original Size - 11" x 14" | Colored pencil & ink on paper | 2023

This piece was created during a time when I felt disconnected and was struggling to find my place. The colorful self-portrait with a pleasant smile was in contrast to my poem about feeling like the 5th option.

Pancakes

Original Size - 11" x 14" | Colored pencil on paper | 2024

When my Dad went out of town, my brother and I were treated to “breakfast for dinner.” Drawn from a photo, I wanted to make a realistic looking stack of pancakes dripping with syrup and butter, the sight of which brings me back to the laughter at that table.

Anxiety

Original Size - 8" x 8" | Acrylics, paper, marker, and thread on canvas | 2024

Given artistic freedom and an 8x8 canvas, I wanted to explore the physical respresentation of the inner me. I cut the heart out and embroidered a cage for protection against further hurt. The butterflies caught in my stomach represented the uneasiness of an anxious mind.

The Underworld

Original Size - 11" x 14" | White charcoal on paper | 2022

We don’t see our skeletons, but they hold us up, and connect together to protect us. I explored this brokenness in this realistic sketch using the Skeleton 3D Anatomy to manipulate the model.

On Top of the World

Original Size - 11" x 14" | Charcoal on paper | 2024

Playing with symmetry and shadow, the top of this piece is a figure from which lines and intricate Celtic knots flow. It illustrates the idea that many of us are filled with contrasting feelings, ideals, and interests that connect the light and dark parts of ourselves.

Geometry

Original Size - 11" x 14" | Pencil on paper | 2024

These geometric shapes are one of my often repeated doodles. I love to play with the depth and shadow while intertwining shapes with and within each other.

Gelato

Original Size - 11" x 14" | Charcoal and oil pastels on paper | 2025

Food is a way to connect people and culture and spread love. I wanted to show the pure joy (and color) that comes from the taste of a handmade gelato.

Kyle

Original Size - 6" x 14" | Oil pastels on paper | 2024

Animals can create safe spaces for people. We see this in therapy dogs in hopspitals and nursing homes and equine therapy in treatment centers. My cat, Kyle, always seems to sense when my day is hard and I tried to capture that even a look between an animal and human can be healing and grounding.

Basilica

Original Size - 6" x 9" | Pencil on paper | 2024

Taking a day trip to Tuscany while staying in Rome, I was able to link urban life with the pastoral life through the similar building techniques and care of construction. This piece was a practice in perspective during a break at a tour stop.

Basil

Original Size - 4" x 6" | Pencil on paper | 2024

This is one of the robot plant cartoons that I dreamed up to play with the idea of nature colliding with techology - in a non-threatening manner.

Octopus

Original Size - 6" x 6" | Pencil on paper | 2024

After reading, "Remarkably Bright Creatures," which chronicles the unlikely friendship between a grieving mother and an Octopus and the ways they helped each other heal, I was inspired to bring Marcellus to life as I imagined him.

Oculus

Original Size - 6" x 9" | Pencil and pen on paper | 2024

The Pantheon in Rome is an architectural feat for many reasons, but the intricate brickwork of the Oculus captivated me from the moment I walked in and I had to capture it with this sketch.
